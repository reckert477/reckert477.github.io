Overview

SpaceBull Nova is a game developed for Putrino Labs operating out of Mount Sinai Hospital. The goal was to create a game for quadriplegic patients featuring the eye tracker and a non-standard clic input, eventually to be interfaced with a BCI (brain controllable interface). By creating an engaging experience, patients would want to interact more with eye-tracker interfaces thus improving their ability at using such devices. Some of the basic design goals and solutions are documented below, but this game does have its own website! Check it out here!

Design Goals & Solutions

Goal: Make players feel powerful as a means to keep them engaged.
Solution: Ensure that every player input has a meaningful and visible gameplay impact. Use VFX to enhance action verbs and give feedback for events that happen as a result of player input.

Goal: Allow for a wide tolerence of player missinput.
Solution: In game design, reducing the need for precise movement and consequences of mistakes. However, still rewarding players who are more precise as encouragement to get better at using the interface. Finally, ensuring that every input has a clear response so that players are not confused as to whether an input has been sent or not.

Goal: Account for dissonance between "Gazing for intent" and "Gazing for understanding".
Solution: Since the nature of gaze is so subconcious, special consideration need to be taken such that optimal gameplay does not conflict with natural bodily instincts. In addition to making objectives eyecatching and reducing punishment for not looking at the objective, rapid prototyping and frequent playtesting helped us find moments of dissonance for our team to resolve.
