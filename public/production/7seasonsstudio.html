Key Responsibilities

  • Coordinate a team of 3 artist and 3 designers to create a cohesive preproduction package, ready for a dedicated development team to build off of.
  • Continually evaluate project needs to identify the next step needed to arrive at a comprehensive idea, and how to get there.
  • Use a variety of tools and heuristics to enable team members to work productively in a way that brings more clarity to the project.
  • Facilitate collaboration between design and art teams, ensuring that neither has an unhealthy amount of control over the project direction.
  • Balance the nebulous nature of concept generation with creating solid deliverables and meeting presentation deadlines.
  • Act as point of contact for the team with university faculty and industry experts. Organize feedback from said parties in a digestible and actionable manner for team members.
  • Act as moral and emotional support for the team, particularly for when setbacks inevitably occur.
  • Distill project findings into a comprehensive report of learnings and takeaways.

Overview

Game preproduction is the least defined aspect of game creation, starting from a blank piece of paper and ending with a concept that has enough clarity that an unrelated team can come in and create a complete and cohesive game off of your preproduction package. The process by which this happens is never the same, and comes down to factors of team members' interests, specialties, working styles, and inevitably the idea that is chosen to polish into a game concept. When progress comes and goes on a whim and project direction can change literally overnight, how does one develop systems to methods to arrive at unified preproduction package? Our team aims to find out by going through the preproduction process ourselves. This project has its own website complete with full postmortem, which you can check out here!
