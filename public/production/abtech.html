Key President Responsibilities

  • Recruit a team of officers to help handle aspects of day to day operation such as finance, personnel, and equipment management.
  • Collaboratively run AB Tech in tandem with organizational board, ensuring smooth operation through frequent and efficient communication.
  • Establish and execute upon long and short term goals for the organization, such as developing strategies for hybrid operation.
  • Build and retain professional relationships with business partners, university staff, and high profile clients.
  • Act as the face of AB Tech in official communications as well as in moments of discord, both internal and external.
  • Track major events and event requests, ensuring that events are not forgotten about and have enough time to properly plan and support.
  • Conduct the smooth handoff of responsibilities to the next president of AB Tech.

Key Event Director Responsibilities

  • Act as a point of contact with clients about their event requests, identifying scope, determining feasibility, and anticipating any potential complications.
  • Recruit and organize a run crew of specialized technicians to handle various audiovisual aspects of the event.
  • Establish and track timeline leading up to the event, factoring for various runcrew dependencies.
  • Coordinate, or delegate the coordination of, equipment with AB Tech inventory.
  • Coordinate between runcrew members, resolving conflicting needs of the departments.
  • Oversee the operation of designated event, handling any issues that arise.
  • Report on how the designated event ran. This consists of noting any difficulties, tips for future similar events, and if necessary creating documentation on how the event went and what to anticipate for future iterations.

Overview

AB Tech acts as an in-house production company for Carnegie Mellon University. AB Tech takes on all sorts of event request ranging from small microphone and speaker for music, to helping run concerts, fashion shows, and even orientation and high profile administration events. Clocking in at over 200 events a year, the logistics required to not only run the organization, but also continually train new technicians, manage in house equipment, and maintain professional relations are immense.

Organizational Breakdown

Due to the high volume of requests and limited working capacity of students, an efficient system of handling event requests is used. When an event request is received, a member of AB Tech will apply to be the event director. Event directors are thus in charge of every aspect of their event going forward, from client communications to putting together a team of technicians to event operation and billing. Part of the responsibilities of the president and organization officers is to ensure that all events are accounted for and that event directors have the tools they need to support the event. However, the two roles are not mutually exclusive. In terms of being an AB Tech technician, officers are no different in applying for event director positions or any technician role required to support an event.

Contributions as President

My term as president was during the COVID pandemic. As such, most of my focus was on retaining institutional knowledge despite a lack of events to train newer members on as well as converting operations to a hybrid format.

  • Spearheaded initiative to create more and higher quality training for basic technician knowledge as well as specialized lighting and audio knowledge.
  • Documented and archived trainings in a way that they can be used for future reference.
  • Personally constructed and ran multiple training sessions, accounting for ways to interactively participate remotely.
  • Improved accessibility of basic AB Tech information to new members.
  • Communicated with university about changing COVID operation policies, generated workflows that enabled AB Tech to get approval for in person operations.
  • Added structure to AB Tech meeting and update cycles to better keep officers and event directors responsible and accountable.

Contributions as Event Director and Technician

Compared to my time as president, I spent significantly more time on the ground supporting events by working with clients and leading a team of technicians to reliably support all natures of event requests.

  • Directed over 50 events for AB Tech, including another 40+ as the primary sound technician.
  • Repeat event director for Lunar Gala, a fashion show joint production with the CMU School of Drama.
    • Worked tightly with Lunar Gala creatives, connecting creative vision with technical feasibility and practicality.
    • Drafted timeline and tracked tasks, accounting for dependencies between various stages of planning.
    • Tracked equipment logistics for in house, borrowed, and rented lighting and rigging fixtures.
    • Created multi-day load in and load out plan down to the half hour in order to maximize work efficiency for technicians and laborers.
  • Event director and/or primary sound technician for 10+ concerts of 200 attendees or greater.
    • Communicated with booking managers, talent managers, and artist production team to track artist needs and work out show schedule.
    • Coordinated rentals of sound, lighting, and staging equipment from external vendors.
    • Coordinated building usage and needs (e.g. power disconnects and modified security hours) with university staff and facilities management services.
    • Drafted precise stage plots and speaker layouts which are optimized for clarity of sound and evenness of sound distribution throughout the room.
